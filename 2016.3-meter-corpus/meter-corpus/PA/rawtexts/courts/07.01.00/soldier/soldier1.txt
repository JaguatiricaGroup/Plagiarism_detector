SERGEANT MAJOR CLEARED OF RACIAL ABUSE<
By Lisa Salmon, PA News<
A sergeant major was today cleared of ill-treating a black soldier and
subjecting him to a torrent of racial abuse.<
A court martial at Catterick, North Yorkshire found Company Sergeant Major
Michael Gleave not guilty of six charges of making racial remarks to or about
Private Roy Carr, and two of ill-treatment.<
CSM Gleave, 39, denied all the charges, which were alleged to have occurred in
1998 while both men were based in Omagh, Northern Ireland with the First
Battalion The Queen's Lancashire Regiment.<
Private Carr, 26, told the hearing he was racially insulted and on two
occasions ordered to run round a camp perimeter fence, despite being medically
unfit.<
Private Carr, the only black soldier in the company, said he kept a diary of
incidents and had received medical treatment as a result of them.<
