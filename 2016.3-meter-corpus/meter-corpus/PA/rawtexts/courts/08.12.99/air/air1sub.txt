FIVE TO FACE TRIAL IN AIR RAGE CASE<
By James Morrison, PA News<
Five Irish tourists charged in connection with an alleged air rage incident
which led to a plane being diverted to the United States were today committed to
face trial at a Crown Court.<
Myles Connors, Francis Coyle, Angela O'Driscoll, Elizabeth O'Driscoll and
Josephine Cooper, all of Thurston Road, Lewisham, south London, were remanded on
unconditional bail by magistrates at Crawley, West Sussex, to enter pleas at
Chichester Crown Court on January 19.<
At an earlier hearing, Connors, 36, and Coyle, 40, each pleaded not guilty to
charges of affray and acting in a manner likely to endanger an aircraft.<
Elizabeth O'Driscoll, 34, Angela O'Driscoll, 39, and Cooper, 19, each denied
being drunk on an aircraft.<
The five defendants were among a group of 12 holidaymakers who were ordered
off an Airtours flight from Gatwick to Montego Bay after it was diverted to the
American city of Norfolk, Virginia, on January 31.<
They flew back to Britain on North West Airlines two days later.<
