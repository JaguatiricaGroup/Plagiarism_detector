MAN ON STRAW DEATH THREAT CHARGE<
By Sinead McIntyre, PA News<
A man charged with threatening to kill Home Secretary Jack Straw was remanded
on bail when he appeared in court today.<
James Notley, 47, of Infirmary Street, Blackburn, Lancashire, appeared before
Blackburn Magistrates on one charge under the Offences Against the Person Act of
threatening to kill both Mr Straw and Manchester Withington Labour MP Keith
Bradley, the Government's deputy chief whip.<
Notley entered no plea and the case was adjourned until December 6.<
He bailed on condition that he did not approach the Home Secretary or go
within 100 metres of the Ivy Street community centre in Blackburn, where Mr
straw holds his constituency surgeries.<
