A SHOPKEEPER accused of murdering a boy was nicked when a bead from the lad's necklace was found over A YEAR later, a jury heard yesterday.

Kevan Roberts, 52, was said to have strangled 12-year-old BMX bike boy Thomas Marshall, of Happisburgh, Norfolk.

His body was dumped 50 miles away. Gay Roberts - who allegedly used the boy for sex - was quizzed about the crime and denied involvement.

But 13 months later cops searched his village store in Eccles, Norfolk, and discovered the chrome bead stuck below his sink plughole.

Roberts denies murder and the trial continues at Norwich Crown Court.
